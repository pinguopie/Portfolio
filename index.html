ABOUT
The heart of Mill Me Not is to raise awareness of an ongoing yet underground issue — Puppy Mill. If you have not heard of the term Puppy Mill, it is an inhumane way of producing a huge amount of puppies at a high speed where profit is achieved at the expense of the dogs. Many pet shops are still supportive of such acts today as it is the fastest way to earn revenues. Hence, it has caught my attention to reach out to potential pet owners in order to lessen Puppy Mill.
BUSINESS MODEL
To help you get a clearer picture of how Puppy Mill works, this is a breakdown of what is going on behind the scene.


LIVING CONDITIONS IN PUPPY MILLS
To maximise the profits, dogs are usually kept at minimal or zero care.
OVERCROWDING
Many dogs are being placed in one cage. This caused them to fight among each other as they try to get more space for each of them.

Length: Measure the length of your dog from nose to tail. When measuring the tail, be sure to add approximately 2-4 inches to the overall length, depending on the size of your dog.

Height: To determine the height of the crate you will need, measure your dog from the top of the head to the ground. If your dog has naturally erect ears, measure from the tip of the ears.

Width: To obtain the width of your crate, add approximately 2 inches for smaller breeds and 4 inches for larger breeds.
CONTAMINATED
They are given very little food and water, or not at all. Sometimes even infested with maggots.
IMPRISONED
Dogs are not usually let out of their cage in the entire Puppy Mill journey. They do not get to play, exercise or socialize.
COMMON HEALTH ISSUES
Due to the lack of care in living condition, it led to various health issues to both the mother dog and its puppies.
OVERGROWN NAILS
In puppy mills, long nails can grow or get get caught around the wire, trapping the dog, or they can grow painfully back into the dog's skin. This just goes to show that the most basic grooming practices are severely neglected.
INFESTED FURS
Because they are confined to such small cages, their fur mats catching urine and feces in the process. This excess of matted fur make the dogs looks bigger than they actually are, and once they are shaved, their emaciated bodies are revealed.
WAITING FOR DEATH
Most of these dogs are not given the necessary medication or a doctors visits. It isn't uncommon for them to go undiagnosed and untreated. The puppies that are ultimately sold to consumers are commonly sick before they even arrive at their forever homes, and sadly, some die shortly after they are purchased and brought home.
HARMFUL IMPACTS ON BREEDING DOGS
INSUFFICIENT RECOVERY TIME
Females are bred repeatedly, usually twice a year, every year, until they can no longer produce puppies. This is incredibly stressful on their bodies but they are viewed as moneymaking machines, as disposable property, not as individuals with inherent worth. Female dogs are commonly bred before it is safe to do so because the earlier they start, the more puppies they will produce in a lifetime.
EMOTIONAL TRAUMA
Mother dogs give birth and live with their puppies through the weaning process, which is usually prematurely forced while the puppies should still be drinking their mother's milk. This early weaning causes emotional trauma to both the mother dog and the puppies as well as health problems in the puppies.
KILLED WHEN NO VALUE
When the breeding dogs' bodies can no longer maintain a high level of productivity, they are destroyed. Puppy millers are no longer profiting from these animals, so they dispose of them in the cheapest way possible. They do it themselves, often on their property, by starving, drowning, shooting, beating, or burying the dogs alive.
AFTERMATH OF PUPPY MILL
Puppy mill dogs require extra attention and training in order to rehabilitate them because the conditions they were brought up in are horrible. Unlike reputable breeders, who take the steps necessary for the comfort of their breeding dogs and puppies, puppy mills treat their dogs as products to be churned out as quickly as possible.
They are usually down with mental health.
GUIDELINES
Although Puppy Mill is an underground issue and confidential to a certain extend, it is still possible to prevent yourself from purchasing puppies from puppy mills.
Breeders who use pressure sales tactics
Sellers who have many types of purebreds or designer breeds
Puppies being sold at less than six weeks old
Breeders who are reluctant to show potential customers the entire premises of breeding
Don't buy from the Internet
ADOPTIONS
Play a part and do not support puppy mill. If you are interested in giving one of the dogs a home, you may consider adoption instead. Together, we can drop the demand and give the homeless dogs a warm home.
